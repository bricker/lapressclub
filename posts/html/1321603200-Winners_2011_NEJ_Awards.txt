<p><strong>A  ENTERTAINMENT JOURNALIST OF THE YEAR, ALL PLATFORMS</strong></p>

<p><strong>Tie: Tara Wallis-Finestone, NBC-LA</strong></p>
<p><strong> Dylan Howard, RadarOnline.com</strong></p>
<p><strong>Judges comments:</strong> <em>As a Senior Producer for NBC4, Tara Wallis-Finestone organizes and writes unique and compelling stories for NBC4 in a fast-moving and highly visual manner. She is a gifted story-teller who writes for the eye. She also writes enterprising stories for NBCLAs website.</em></p>
<p><strong>Judges comments: </strong><em>In the world of celebrity and entertainment news, even mainstream media couldnt ignore exclusive stories broken under Dylan Howards tenure as senior executive editor of RadarOnline.</em></p>
<p><em>From the hate-filled rants of Mel Gibson, exclusive interviews with Charlie Sheen during his meltdown, to the naming of the mother of Schwarzeneggers love child, Howard has become the go-to guy for authoritative show biz news and analysis on cable and over-the air television.</em></p>

<p><strong>B1  TV NEWS</strong></p>
<p>George Pennacchio, Cheryl Diano, KABC-TV, Goodbye, Columbo</p>
<p><strong>Judges comments:</strong> <em>Pennacchio weaves in years of past interview with Peter Falk in creating a riveting obituary and tribute to one of TVs best-loved actors, Peter Falk. This well-crafted piece shows the value of beat reporting, of tapping long-forgotten video and notes to add depth and appeal to a subject. The result shows the arc of Falks life, from the early days of film roles in B-pictures to the last days when he was fighting dementia.</em></p>

<p><strong>C1  RADIO NEWS </strong></p>
<p>Sanden Totten, KPCC, China Hollywood</p>
<p><strong>Judges comments:</strong> <em>This richly-packed report on a $30 million, first-time-ever investment by China into a big Hollywood feature film  an epic love story set in ancient China  has a casual, airy feel that makes for good listening. But it delves into big ideas about how China has not yet perfected the art of the widely appealing blockbuster, so its partnering with Hollywood  all with an eye to boosting its influence in the realm of cultural capital and eventually assuring that the Western media isnt the only game in film.</em></p>
<p>2<sup>nd</sup>: Carole Zimmer, Bloomberg News, New Economics of Hollywood</p>
<p>HM: Alex Cohen, KPCC, Porn Industry</p>

<p><strong>C2  RADIO PERSONALITY PROFILE </strong></p>
<p>Larry Mantle, Airtalk with Larry Mantle, KPCC, Dick Van Dyke</p>
<p><strong>Judges comments:</strong> <em>Dick Van Dyke sure doesnt sound 84 in this sparkling chat that Larry Mantle, who turns this interview into a laughter-filled talk between friends. The conversation turns Van Dykes dozens of TV and movie hits to his lifelong collaborators Morey Amsterdam and Rose Marie. Van Dyke is jovial and fresh, bragging about his new band, with whom he is having a romp. Funny, heartwarming stuff. </em></p>
<p>2<sup>nd</sup>: Adolfo Guzman-Lopez, KPCC, East LA Musician</p>
<p>HM: Alex Cohen, KPCC, Gay Guru</p>

<p><strong>C3  RADIO FEATURE </strong></p>
<p>Brian Lauritzen and Gail Eichenthal, Classical KUSC, LA Phil in Europe</p>
<p><strong>Judges comments:</strong> <em>A vibrant tale that reports on Dudamel &amp; Co. as Dudamel explores ideas with, and trains, young orchestra students in London. The story is interspersed with fantastic bits of symphonic sound, Dudamels high-charged patter, and music students who love the symphony practicing and chuckling at themselves  and at Dudamel. As Lauritzen says it was intense.</em></p>
<p>2<sup>nd</sup>: John DeSando and Kristin Dreyer Kramer, WCBE, Its Movie Times 2010 Year in Review</p>
<p>HM: Carole Zimmer, Mark Mills, Al Mayers, WBBR Bloomberg Radio, Spiderman Takes a Dive</p>

<p><strong>C4  RADIO CRITIC</strong></p>
<p>Kenneth Turan and Gail Eichenthal, Classical KUSC, Kenneth Turans Art Alive Film Reports</p>
<p><strong>Judges comments: </strong><em>Turan and Eichenthal make a smooth team, with Turan sharing his pearls of wisdom and asides about his favorite and least-favorite moments in the latest movies as Eichenthal asks him pithy questions. Turans quirky voice is by no means a natural for radio, but he knows so much  and shares so much in such a short bit of time  that it really doesnt matter. Engaging and worthwhile.</em></p>
<p>2<sup>nd</sup>: John DeSando and Kristin Dreyer Kramer, WCBE (Columbus), Nights and Weekends</p>
