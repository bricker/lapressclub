<p>The tables will be turned when the legendary editor-in-chief of Playboy Magazine, <strong>Hugh M</strong>. <strong>Hefner,</strong> is interviewed onstage by NBC4 news anchor <strong>Robert Kovacik</strong> in the incisive and entertaining style pioneered by the popular mens magazine over 50 years ago.</p>
<p>The legendary comic and civil rights icon <strong>Dick Gregory</strong> is flying in from Washington D.C. to pay a special tribute the man who gave him his first break, Mr. Hefner.</p>
<p>It takes place Thursday, Nov. 17 at the <strong>Biltmore Bowl</strong> in Los Angeles as part of the Los Angeles Press Clubs Award Dinner for the finalists in the Clubs <strong>4<sup>th</sup> Annual National Entertainment</strong> <strong>Awards</strong> competition that attracted hundreds of entries from all over the U.S.</p>
<p>The live interview follows Hefners acceptance of a <strong>Lifetime Achievement Award</strong> in entertainment journalism. The LA Press Club Board cited Hefners huge, unmatched footprint on popular culture and his unflinching support of First Amendment rights for the average citizen as well as the press.</p>
<p>Locally, Hefner help save the landmark Hollywood Signtwice, is a major contributor to USCs Film School and has produced the Playboy Jazz Festival at the Hollywood Bowl for 34 years.</p>
<p>Tickets for the reception, dinner and silent auction are available onlineor call Diana @ 323.669..8081 to purchase tables or buy ads for the Tribute Book.</p>
<p>Twenty-two of the best entertainment reporters and photographers in the country will take home coveted first-place trophies for their finest work in 2011 and part of 2010. Bloomber, The Hollywood Reporter, Parade Magazine, Deadline Hollywood, Los Angeles Times, RadarOnline, LA Weekly, NBC4, Antelope Valley Press, KPCC, ABC7, Geekweek.com, Jewish Journal of LA and KUSC are represented by multiple finalists. Other single finalists file for Vanity Fair, TheWrap and WCBE, among others.</p>
<p>The winner of the highly competitive category of entertainment journalist of the year will receive a $500 check.</p>
<p>There is a $20 dollar valet service at the Biltmore Hotel or you can park in Pershing Square for $6.60.</p>
