Press Club Dedicates Gala to a Firefighter's Firefighter
-----------------------------------------------

<div class="post-bodycopy clearfix">
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong><a href="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/jim-perry-copy.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-834" title="jim perry copy" src="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/jim-perry-copy.jpg" alt="" width="109" height="111"></a>James Arthur Perry, 12/30/39 to 12/3/09</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Editor, <em>The Los Angeles Firefighter</em></strong></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Jim Perry was a staunch supporter of the Los Angeles Press Club for nearly 40 years and his publications won many LAPC awards over the decades.<span id="more-731"></span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">
</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">But he was better known by his fellow firemen as a “firefighter’s firefighter.” He was a founding father of Local 112, United Firefighters of Los Angeles City at a time when union activity was discouraged by the Department.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Jim’s editorials for the Los Angeles Firefighter regularly “tackled hot-button issues without mincing words.” Sarah Kovitch’s obituary for the newspaper credited Perry with “rousing readers into political action.”</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">In 1953, President Kennedy awarded him the National Red Cross Life Saving Award for the LAFD’s first successful CPR.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">-30-</p>
