Big Night for Entertainment Journalism; Dick Gregory Pays Tribute to Hugh Hefner
-----------------------------------------------

<div class="post-bodycopy clearfix">
<p><a href="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image011.jpg"><br>
</a><a href="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image013.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1975" title="SONY DSC" src="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image013-212x300.jpg" alt="" width="212" height="300"></a><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1973" title="SONY DSC" src="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image001-201x300.jpg" alt="" width="201" height="300"></p>
<p>At Thursday’s LA Press Club’s 4th Annual National Entertainment Journalism Awards, The Hollywood Reporter walked off with four first place trophies, including best entertainment publication.</p>
<p>There was a tie for Entertainment Journalist of the Year. Tara Wallis-Finestone, senior producer for NBC4-LA, and Dylan Howard, senior executive editor of RadarOnline, each took home a $500 check. The award was sponsored by Alexandra and Sidney Sheldon. Alexandra is the widow of the late best-selling novelist, screen and TV writer.</p>
<p><a href="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image016.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1981" title="image016" src="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image016-300x199.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="199"></a></p>
<p>Media outlets that had first place winners in three categories included Los Angeles Times, LA Weekly, KPCC and RadarOnline.</p>
<p><span id="more-1957"></span></p>
<p><a href="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image017.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1982" title="image017" src="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image017-300x199.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="199"></a><a href="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image015.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1980" title="image015" src="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/image015-300x199.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="199"></a></p>
<p>Comedian and civil rights icon Dick Gregory lauded Hugh M. Hefner, who accepted the Los Angeles Press Club’s Lifetime Achievement Award as “Champion of the First Amendment.”</p>
<p>Hefner booked Gregory at his Chicago Playboy Club in 1961, the first black comic to play in a white club. Gregory said Hefner made it possible for America to “hear the voices of Moms Mabley, Richard Pryor, Red Foxx and Eddie Murphy.”</p>
<p>From contractmusic.com</p>
<p>Hefner shared his special evening with his son Cooper and girlfriend Anna Sophia Berglund….In a subsequent post on his Twitter.com page, the veteran publisher wrote, “The LA Press Club’s Lifetime Achievement dinner proved to be a really special, quite emotional evening for me. The [award] had even more meaning because family &amp; friends were there to share the night.</p>
<p>“Dick Gregory’s personal tribute was the real highlight of the evening for me. He flew across the country to be here.”</p>
<p>The awards gala was held at the Biltmore Bowl. More than 200 attended the dinner, including Laker owner Dr. Jerry Buss and Berry Gordy who founded Motown records.</p>
