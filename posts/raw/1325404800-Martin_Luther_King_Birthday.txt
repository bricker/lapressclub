<p><strong>What if Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. had Tweeted the Civil Rights Movement</strong><span class="Apple-style-span" style="color: #ff0000;"><strong>   </strong></span></p>
<p>On Thursday Jan. 12, the Los Angeles Press Club presented a prestigious panel discussion of how Dr. King might have used social media in the civil rights movement.</p>
<p>The event was in honor of Dr. King’s Birthday, Monday Jan. 16.</p>
<p><strong><span id="more-2033"></span></strong></p>
<p>Panelists:</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.dianeewatson.com/">Diane Watson</a></strong> – Former U.S. Congresswoman<br>
<a href="http://www.presstelegram.com/news/ci_18171410"><strong>Robert Farrell</strong> </a>– Former L.A. City Councilman and Freedom Rider<br>
<a href="https://www.facebook.com/wdsmart?sk=info"><strong>Rev. William D. Smart Jr.</strong> </a>– Chair, SCLC-LA and Union Organizer<br>
<a href="http://www.rccfellowship.org/v3/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=79:co-pastor-rev-dr-lewis-e-logan-ii&amp;catid=39:staff-bios&amp;Itemid=157"><strong>Rev. Dr. Lewis E. Logan II</strong> </a>–Pastor &amp; Community Organizer<br>
<strong><a href="http://charlene.blogs.finalcall.com/">Charlene Muhammad</a></strong> – Journalist and Blogger<br>
<strong><a href="http://www.jasmynecannick.com/blog/">Jasmyne Cannick</a></strong> – Journalist and Blogger</p>
<p>Moderated by<br>
<strong><a href="http://anthonysamad.com/">Dr. Anthony Asadullah Samad</a></strong> – Author/Columnist/Scholar</p>
