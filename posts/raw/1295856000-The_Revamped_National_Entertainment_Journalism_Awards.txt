The Revamped National Entertainment Journalism Awards
-----------------------------------------------

<div class="post-bodycopy clearfix">
<p>NEW DATES *** NEW CATEGORIES *** CASH PRIZES *** NEW VENUE</p>
<p>The Los Angeles Press Club has a new timeline for its 4<sup>th</sup> National Entertainment Journalism Awards celebration as it fine-tunes and upgrades this unique competition.</p>
<p>The new Call for Entries date is July 1<sup>st</sup> with a submission deadline of September 1<sup>st</sup>.  The Awards party will be held mid-November at a new venue– the exact date and location will be announced soon.<span id="more-1216"></span></p>
<p>A <em>Best of Show</em> cash prize will be featured along with expanded categories including best tweet or tweets and best Facebook presence.</p>
<p>The top award for career achievement will also be announced. Last year’s award went to KTLA entertainment reporter <strong>Sam Rubin</strong>. Film critic<strong> Joe Morgenstern</strong>, of KCRW and Wall Street Journal, was honored 2009.</p>
<p>To qualify, all work submitted must have been published or broadcast between January 1st, 2010, and June 30th, 2011.</p>
<p>The National Entertainment Journalism Awards were founded in 2008 to recognize the finest work from US-based entertainment reporters and editors, and theater, film, and television critics in all media – print, radio, TV, and online. This includes international journalists working in a language other than English.</p>
