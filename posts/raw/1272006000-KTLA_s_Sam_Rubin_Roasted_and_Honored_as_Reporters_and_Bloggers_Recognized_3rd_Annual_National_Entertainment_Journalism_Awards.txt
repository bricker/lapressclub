<div><span style="color: #0033ff;"><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;"><img src="http://ih.constantcontact.com/fs007/1102748757357/img/213.jpg" border="0" alt="MDA Pix 1" width="202" height="147" align="left">HO</span><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;"><strong>LLYWOOD, CA. </strong>The Detroit Free Press and Freep.com’s “Motown at 50″ online entertainment project won “<strong>Best of Show</strong>” and a $500 check at the Los Angeles Press Club’s 3<sup>rd</sup> Annual National Entertainment Journalism Awards Thursday night in Hollywood. </span></span></span></div>
<div>
<div><strong><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;"> </span></strong></div>
<div>
<p><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;">The best entertainment website beat out first place winners in 15 other categories, from show business reporters and media organizations across the nation–from Alaska to Kansas City.</span></p>
<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;">The LA Weekly’s chief film critic <strong>Scott Foundas</strong>, now with the New York Film Festival, walked away with two awards, including Print Critic of the Year.  KABC Channel 7′s <strong>George Pennacchio</strong> also scored two wins in the TV news and feature categories. </span></div>
</div>
<div>KTLA entertainment reporter <strong>Sam Rubin </strong>was “roasted” by screenwriter <strong>Stephen Mazur </strong>and KTLA anchor <strong>Jessica Holmes</strong> and received the top individual award for career achievement.  Last year’s award went to Joe Morgenstern, KCRW and Wall Street Journal film critic.</div>
<p><span id="more-654"></span></p>
</div>
<p><span><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;">Below are the NEJ 2010 Winners presented by Los Angeles Press Club. .</span></span></p>
<div>
<div>
<div>
<div>

<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;"> </span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;">A1.  Print News </span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;">Winner:  <strong>Kyle Hopkins</strong>, Anchorage Daily News, “E.T. go Nome?”</span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;"> </span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;">A2a. Print Feature or Series over 1,000 Words </span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;"> </span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;">Winner:  <strong>Richard Siklos</strong>, Fortune, “The Fight Over Michael’s Millions”</span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Garamond, Times New Roman, Times, serif;">Second Place: Anthony Effinger and Daniel Taub, Bloomberg Markets, “All Shook Up”</span></div>
