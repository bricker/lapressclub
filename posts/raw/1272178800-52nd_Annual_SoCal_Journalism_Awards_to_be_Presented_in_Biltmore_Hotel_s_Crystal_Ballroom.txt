52nd Annual SoCal Journalism Awards to be Presented in Biltmore Hotel's Crystal Ballroom
-----------------------------------------------

<div class="post-bodycopy clearfix">
<p><a href="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/anderson_cooper_copy.gif"><img class="alignright" title="anderson_cooper_copy" src="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/anderson_cooper_copy-241x300.gif" alt="CNN anchor Anderson Cooper to receive President's Award, June 27" width="69" height="87"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"> </span></span></a></p>
<div id="attachment_702" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 136px">
<a href="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/la_crystalballroom2.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-702 " title="la_crystalballroom2" src="http://lapressclub.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/la_crystalballroom2-300x197.jpg" alt="Crystal Ballroom, Biltmore Hotel" width="126" height="83"></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Crystal Ballroom, Biltmore Hotel</p>
</div>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">When CNN’s <strong>Anderson Cooper </strong>is presented the Los Angeles Press Club’s President’s Award for impact on the media, he will be in the legendary Crystal Ballroom at the Millennium Biltmore, where the Oscars were born.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">An expected 400 journalists and their invited guests will gather on the evening of June 27th to find out who among them have won in the 52nd Annual Southern California Journalism Awards competition. </span></span></p>
<div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">More than 500 entries from print, online, broadcasting and college media are now being judged by press clubs around the </span></span><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">country<span id="more-674"></span>…</span></span>
</div>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"> </span></span><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;">Dave Bryan, </span></strong></span></strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">a TV reporter for more than 30 years at KTTV and now at CBS2/KCAL9</span></span><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">, will receive the <strong>Joseph M. Quinn Award</strong> for lifetime achievement.</span></span></span></span></p>
<div>
<span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong> </strong></span></span><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">NPR’s </span></span></span><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;">Anne Garrels </span></strong></span></strong></span></strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">is the 2010 recipient of the</span></span><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"> Daniel Pearl Award</span></strong></span></strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"> for courage and integrity in journalism in recognition of her selflessness in pursuing difficult, often dangerous stories over the years around the world.</span></span><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"> The award will be presented by </span></span><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;">Judea Pearl, </span></strong></span></strong><span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">father of the late Wall Street Journal reporter killed by Pakistani extremists in 2002. </span></span></span>
</div>
<div><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong> </strong></span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Arial;"> </span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Arial;">“It’s a great lineup for a great night in Southern California journalism,” <strong>says the </strong>new Press Club President <strong>Will Lewis, </strong>a public radio veteran based at KCRW-FM. “It proves the point that outstanding journalism continues despite unprecedented difficulties for all news media.”</span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Arial;"> </span></div>
<div><span style="font-family: Arial;">The Club will honor quality reporting in Southern California for 2009. For the first time, it will offer a $1,000 check for “Best of Show” Journalist of the Year.</span></div>
<p>According to Wikipedia,The Biltmore “is known for once being a home to the Oscars. The Academy of Motion Picture Arts &amp; Sciences was founded at a luncheon banquet in the Crystal Ballroom in May 1927, when guests like Louis B. Mayer met to discuss plans for presenting achievement awards…. Legend has it that MGM art director Cederic Gibbons, immediately grabbed a Biltmore napkin and sketched the design for the Oscar….. In 1977 Bob Hope hosted the Academy’s 50th Anniversary banquet in the same room.”</p>
