Results of LA Press Club's December Elections
-----------------------------------------------

<div class="post-bodycopy clearfix">
<div><span style="font-size: small;"> <span id="more-1183"></span>The Los Angeles Press Club announces the results of its December election for the 2011 Board of Directors.</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: small;">Newly elected are <strong>Tony</strong> <strong>Castro</strong>, Daily News;<strong> Eric Leonard</strong>, KFI News; <strong>Martha Sarabia</strong>, La Opinion and <strong>Sharon Waxman</strong>, The Wrap.  Incumbents reelected are:<strong>John Amato</strong>, Crooks&amp;Liars; <strong>Diana Ljungaeus</strong>, international journalist and <strong>Jon Regardie</strong>, Downtown News.</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: small;">Continuing board members are <strong>Beth Barrett</strong>, investigative reporter; <strong>Jane Engle</strong>, LA Times; <strong>Rory Johnston</strong>, freelance; <strong>Fred Mamoun</strong>, KNBC-4 News, <strong>Will Lewis</strong>, KCRW emeritus; <strong>Jill Stewart</strong>, LA Weekly and <strong>Chris Woodyard</strong>, USA Today.</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: small;">“The  incoming board represents all news platforms-the legacy media and the  Internet,” said current Club president, Will Lewis. “And with the  election of  Ms. Sarabria, we’ve added the Spanish language press.”</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: small;">The board will elect a new slate of officers at its January 18<sup>th</sup> meeting.</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: small;">Only  members who are fulltime journalists participate in the annual  election. Active members also approved changes in the Club’s bylaws  designed to streamline board governance.</span></div>
