<p><a title="Patch Hits South Pasadena, Press Club" href="http://www.mediabistro.com/fishbowlla/patch-south-pasadena-marcia-parker-press-club_b12211">Patch Hits South Pasadena, Press Club</a></p>
<p><span id="more-1039"></span>In these times of journalistic layoffs and media conglomerate belt-tightening, the rapid, ongoing Southern California expansion of community news hub venture <a title="Patch.com" href="http://www.patch.com/">Patch.com</a> seems almost surreal. Yesterday, after some last-minute technical hiccups, the AOL seed-funded enterprise turned on the newest of its area websites, <a href="http://southpasadena.patch.com/" target="_">South Pasadena Patch</a>, the same day the L.A. Press Club <a title="announced" href="../uncategorized/aol-patch-editorial-director-at-the-press-club-thursday-1014/">announced</a> that San Francisco-based Patch west coast editorial director <strong><a href="http://www.mediabistro.com/Marcia-Parker-profile.html">Marcia Parker</a></strong> (pictured) will be flying down to address the organization’s annual meeting on Thursday, October 14th.<em> Read more</em>.</p>

<p><strong>Marcia Parker, AOL Patch’s West Coast Honcho, Says The News Site Will Address L.A.’s Diversity By Using Google Translate</strong></p>
<p>By <a href="http://blogs.laweekly.com/author.php?author_id=1397">Dennis Romero</a>, Fri., Oct. 15 2010 @ 1:28PM</p>
<p>In the last few months <a href="http://www.laweekly.com/2010-09-30/news/patch-the-walmart-of-news/" target="_blank">AOL’s “hyper-local” news sites called Patch</a> have been hard to ignore. Not only have they moved into some of Los Angeles County’s more-affluent communities, but they’ve been <a href="http://laist.com/2010/10/15/aols_patch_launches_2_san_gabriel_v.php" target="_blank">hiring journalists</a> at a time when some of the <a href="http://www.laobserved.com/archive/2010/10/la_times_adds_two_film_wr.php" target="_blank">best scribes in town</a> have been surviving on unemployment.<em> Read more</em>.</p>
